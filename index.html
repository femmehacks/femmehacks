February 19th - 20th, 2016

University of Pennsylvania: The 2nd Floor of Jon M. Huntsman Hall

Hosted by the Women in Computer Science chapter @ Penn WiCS.

FemmeHacks is an all-women collegiate hackathon that aims to create a welcoming environment for women in tech. FemmeHacks is available to all women, beginner to experienced, and allows students to explore their passion for tech by building on web, mobile and hardware to create projects over the weekend. This is FemmeHacks’ second year, and we can't wait to make it our best yet.

Unforunately, registration is now closed. But we'll see you next year at FemmeHacks '17!

FAQ

Who can come?

- Any college or high student student in the Philly area is welcome. No experience is required!

Where is it?

MBA Cafe - Jon M. Huntsman Hall
@ the University of Pennsylvania

When is it?

- The actual hackathon will take place on Saturday, February 20th, but there will be team matching and hackathon-startup sessions on Friday.

What should I bring?

- A computer, charger, and enthusiasm :D

Is it okay if I'm not an ~experienced~ hacker?

- Yes. There will be tons of mentors, workshops, and other fellow attendees to help you out!

How many people can I take in a team?

- Max of 4 people, please.

How much does it cost?

- Nothing! Food, drinks, and $w@g provided. Note that there is no travel reimbursement or housing at this time.

I don't have a team. Can I still come?

- Absolutely! You will get the chance to make one on Friday night.

Ok, I'm almost convinced.

- Come make amazing memories + friends, network with awesome companies, and develop awesome projects to showcase your skillz.

Can we talk about diversity?

- Uhm, yes please!!! FemmeHacks values diversity of age, race, economic status, gender expression, size, physical ability, developmental ability, learning styles, nationality, religion, thought, citizenship status, and sexual orientation. We welcome all disciplines and interests, experience levels, and skill sets. We promote respect and do not tolerate racism, sexism, homophobia, or other discriminatory behavior or expression. FemmeHacks acknowledges the complicated nature of the word “women,” knowing it may not work for many, and can feel unwelcoming or alienating for some. We use * to specifically and intentionally welcome trans and cis women, as well as nonbinary and gender non-conforming folks :). Equality for all, y'all.
